## Overview

The InternSwipe team follows three recurring ceremonies to maintain alignment, demonstrate progress, and continuously improve. These ceremonies are intentionally lightweight to maximize the time available for building, while providing enough structure to ensure that no team member falls behind or works in isolation. Every team member is expected to participate in all three ceremonies every week without exception.

## Ceremony summary

| Ceremony | Frequency | Duration | Format | Purpose | Status |
|----------|-----------|----------|--------|---------|--------|
| Async standup | Every session day (4 times per week) | 10 minutes | Written post in team channel | Share progress, declare intent, and surface blockers before starting work. | **done** — process defined and adopted |
| Review and demo | End of Session D each week | 20 minutes | Live browser demo | Each pair demonstrates what they built during the week to the full team. | **done** — process defined and adopted |
| Retrospective | Immediately after review and demo | 15 minutes | Structured discussion | Reflect on what went well, what could improve, and commit to one action item per person. | **done** — process defined and adopted |

## Async standup

The async standup is a written post that every team member submits in the team communication channel before beginning work on each session day. The purpose of the standup is to share progress, declare what will be worked on during the current session, and surface any blockers that could prevent forward movement. Because the team does not meet synchronously at the start of every session, the written format ensures that everyone has visibility into what their teammates are doing.

Each standup note must answer the following three questions:

1. What was completed in the previous session?
2. What will be worked on in the current session?
3. Are there any blockers that could prevent progress?

The standup should take no more than 10 minutes to write. Answers should be specific and actionable. For example, "worked on backend stuff" is not acceptable; "implemented the `GET /api/jobs` endpoint with pagination and wrote two unit tests" is. If there are no blockers, the team member should explicitly state "No blockers" rather than leaving the field blank.

## Review and demo

The review and demo takes place at the end of Session D each week and lasts approximately 20 minutes. During this ceremony, each pairing group demonstrates what they built during the week in a live browser. The demo must show working features in the deployed Vercel preview or local development environment -- slides, screenshots, or code walkthroughs do not satisfy the demo requirement.

The demo format is as follows:

1. Each pair takes a turn presenting their work for the week.
2. The presenter navigates through the feature in a live browser while narrating what is happening.
3. Other team members observe and ask questions or raise concerns.
4. If a deliverable is not demo-able, the pair must explain why and state what is needed to complete it.

The review and demo serves two purposes: it provides accountability by requiring every pair to show tangible progress each week, and it gives the full team visibility into the state of the entire application rather than only the parts they personally worked on.

## Retrospective

The retrospective takes place immediately after the review and demo at the end of Session D and lasts approximately 15 minutes. The purpose of the retrospective is to reflect on the week's work and identify one concrete improvement that each team member will act on during the following week.

Each team member shares three items:

1. What went well during the week that the team should continue doing.
2. What could be improved, including process friction, communication gaps, or technical debt.
3. One specific action item that the team member commits to completing in the next week to address an identified improvement.

Action items must be specific, measurable, and achievable within a single week. For example, "communicate better" is not an acceptable action item; "post standup notes within 30 minutes of session start every day" is. Action items from the previous week's retrospective should be reviewed at the start of each new retrospective to check whether they were completed.
